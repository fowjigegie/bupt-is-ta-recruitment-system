Electromagnetic Waves
o Propagation vector or Poynting vector: is the power flow density of the EM
wave, vector P = E × H in the direction of propagation, and measured in
watts per square meter.
o Electromagnetic field: can be described in terms of the electric and/or magnetic
field strength at that location.
o Like any wave-related phenomenon, electromagnetic energy can be
characterized by a wavelength and/or frequency.
▪ Frequency f: is defined as a number of cycles, or periods, per unit of time and is
measured in hertz (Hz).
▪ The wavelength λ: is the distance by which the phase of the sinusoidal wave
changes by 2π radians, so we can say: kλ = 2π, w = kc, and f = w/2π.
▪ Angular frequency w: is a rotational unit of angular frequency 2πf with units in
radians per second (rad/s).

RF Spectrum and Electromagnetic Signal Transmission 
(Cont’D)
o LF: 30kHz~300kHz, usually passive tags, communication range < 1m, not be
disturbed by any material except for metal, typical work frequency:
125kHz~134kHz
o HF: 3MHz~30MHz，usually passive tags, communication range < 1m,
typical work frequency: 13.56MHz
o UHF: 300MHz~3GHz, passive or active tags, communication range > 1m,
cannot go through many materials, especially for water, dust, fog and other
suspended particulate materials, typical work frequency: 433MHz ，
860MHz~960MHz
o SHF： passive or active tags, communication range > 10m at most, typical
work frequency: 2.45GHz，5.8GHz（microwave signal）

o Any object that receives certain electromagnetic waves will transmit the same
series of electromagnetic waves.
o The electromagnetic waves modulated by the receiver can return to the
antenna of the transmitter, and produce a recognizable signal, which is called
backscatter signal.

Basic Antenna Parameters
o A transmitting antenna is a device used to transform an RF signal, traveling
on a conductor, into an electromagnetic wave in free space.
o A receiving antenna performs an inverse function.
o Antennas demonstrate a property known as reciprocity, which means that an
antenna will maintain the same characteristics regardless if it is transmitting or
receiving.
o Most antennas are passive resonant devices, which operate efficiently over
a relatively narrow frequency band.
o When a signal is fed into an antenna, the antenna will emit radiation
distributed in space in a certain way.
o A graphical representation of the relative angular distribution of the radiated
power in space is called a radiation pattern.

Antenna Polarization and Polarization Diversity
o Polarization is a physical phenomenon of radio signal propagation and refers to
the orientation of the electric field vector in the radiated wave.
o If the vector appears to rotate with time, then the wave is elliptically polarized.
o The ellipse so described may vary in ellipticity from a circle to a straight line,
or from circular to linear polarization.
o So, in the general sense, all polarization may be considered to be elliptical.

Linear Polarization and Circle Polarization
o In the figure, the electromagnetic vector represents the direction of the

o The direction of the electric field and the magnetic field are perpendicular to

For different applications of RFID systems, the first thing to
be considered is RFID carrier frequency. Avoiding conflicts is
the basis for the normal operation of RFID systems; UHF
signals are suitable for long-range identification, while LF
signals are suitable for short-range applications.
It also needs carefully choice for the forward link and
reverse link signal modulation encoding situation. Passive
and semi-active tags, encoded to be able to provide stable
energy; in more complex wireless environment, anti-
interference ability should be strong.

The link budget is an important parameter related to the
scope of application of the RFID system. The maximum
transmission power, signal attenuation and path loss, tag
activation energy and other factors nee to be considered.
Long-distance transmission, take the link budget distance
limit into consideration; close communication, reduce the
transmission power to reduce the communication distance.

===== PAGE 57 =====
RFID system is running in the actual environment, the
transmission interference of which is also much more
complex than the theoretical analysis. Such as real noise,
obstruction, signal absorption, signal scattering, multipath
effects, and so on. Noise interferes the recognition and
transmission of the signal. Signal absorption interferes the
transmission range of RFID system. Signal scattering makes
RFID signal recognition unstable. Some areas that should not
be covered may be misread. Multipath effects can cause erratic
changes in signal strength, resulting in instability in the range
of tag identification.
