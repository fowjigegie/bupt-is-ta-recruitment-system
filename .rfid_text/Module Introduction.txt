Module Description
5
• Provide broad knowledge of RFID architecture, identification, standard 
protocol, design methods, and its practical concerns.
• Equip the students with general knowledge of data transmission techniques, 
methods of designing typical RFID systems, RFID tags, tag encoding 
mechanisms, link budget, anti-collision algorithms, the EPCglobal 
architecture, the RFID performance analysis and other related topics to 
sensors and RFID technology.
• This module lays a good foundation for further study as it discusses 
innovative research ideas and exploration of RFID systems.

===== PAGE 6 =====
Module Aims
6
• Aim 1: Provide students with a comprehensive understanding of basic RFID 
technological aspects including system components, wireless communication 
principles, and applications. 
• Aim 2: Equip students with analytical skills through analysis of 
standardization/normalization and security of RFID systems.
• Aim 3: Develop students’ ability to design RFID systems by exploring design and 
testing methodologies and metrics. 
• Aim 4: Develop students’ critical analysis and evaluation skills by exploring and 
analyzing new RFID developments trends through research papers.
• Aim 5: Develop students’ practical skills by designing real IoT projects using RFID 
tags/reader and microcontrollers (e.g. Arduino) and developing programming codes 
on RFID (e.g. Arduino IDE). 

===== PAGE 7 =====
Learning Outcomes
7
Academic Content: 
By the end of this module, the students will be able to:
• Explain the basic principles and working principles of sensors and wireless 
identification technology.
• Acquire comprehensive knowledge on data transmission techniques, RFID 
system architecture, and wireless communication principles.
• Analyse EPCglobal standard protocol and design methodologies of RFID 
systems.
• Interpret and evaluate the performance of sensors and RFID systems in 
practice.
• Evaluate the most innovative research ideas and practical concerns of RFID 
systems.

===== PAGE 8 =====
Transferrable Skills
8
Disciplinary Skills: By the end of this module, the students will be able to:
• Explore the use of technical literature and other information sources.
• Carry out research in the field of sensors and RFID systems and apply the 
findings effectively in engineering projects.
• Transform theoretical knowledge into practical platform by designing the 
hardware and software of a specific sensors and RFID related projects.
Graduate Attributes:
• Develop technical skills including creativity, evaluation, and troubleshooting.
• Develop teamwork and oral and written communication skills.
• Familiarize with professional environment and recognize its limitations.
