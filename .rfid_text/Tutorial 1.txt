There are four major frequency ranges at which RFID systems operate. As a rule of thumb, low-frequency systems are distinguished by short reading ranges, slow read speeds, and lower cost. Higher-frequency RFID systems are used where longer read ranges and fast reading speeds are required, such as for vehicle tracking and automated toll collection. Resonant Frequency Tuning Passive RFID tags (LF at 125 kHz) use an LC circuit (Inductor + Capacitor) to harvest energy from the reader’s magnetic field. For the tag to work, the circuit must be tuned to the exact frequency of the reader. If the frequency of the circuit does not march the reader, the tag cannot extract enough power.

Case Study 1: The Automated Logistics Hub (Warehouse & Conveyor)  § The Problem: A distribution centre needs to track thousands of low-cost retail items moving on a conveyor belt into cardboard boxes. Frequency: Choose the best frequency for high-speed, bulk-inventory scanning. 2. Tag Type: Should you use passive or active tags? Consider the cost-per-item for retail goods. 3. Reader Type: Should you use fixed or handheld readers to automate the loading bay? 4. Antenna Configuration: Design an antenna setup for the conveyor belt to ensure not tags are missed due to “blind spots” (tag orientation). 5.
